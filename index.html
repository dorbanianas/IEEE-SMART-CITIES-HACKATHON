About the event

This year, IEEE Student Branch is organizing its biggest event, that will be a hackathon under the theme: “Smart cities”. As the title suggests, our hackathon’s goal will be to value the adoption of new technologies to improve the digital services of the cities. This event will target engineering students of Moroccan institutions and universities. It’s a great opportunity where participants can showcase their creativity, innovation and endurance along with a teamwork mindset to present innovative solutions that revolve around diverse topics: industry, environment, agriculture, health, education.... Our event will start with workshops as webinars and video conferences presented by highly qualified guests and mentors to better introduce our participants to the hackathon’s concept and its process, ensure equal opportunities and minimize the discrepancy between our groups’ skills. After three days of competition, a specialized jury committee will select the best projects and our winners will receive the promised prizes.

About IEEE


IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) is a non- profit professional association. It results from merging the Ameri- can Institute of Electrical Engineers and the Institute of Radio En- gineers. Consisting of electrical engineers, computer scientists, and experts in telecommunications and other fields, IEEE is the largest professional and technological association globally. We're talking about more than 419,000 members, 124,442 of whom are students, according to the latest statistics of 2021. Branching out into several branches from over 160 countries, IEEE unifies all its sub-units under one vision with clear and prom- ising goals: the promotion of knowledge in electrical, electronic, and computer engineering. To give you a clearer idea of the weight of this organization, its documentation alone has more than 5 million documents, with an average of 20,000 documents added every month.
